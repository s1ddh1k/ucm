## Design Context

### Users
UCM Desktop is for technical operators building and supervising an autonomous software factory. The primary users are software engineers or founders who need to move between mission setup, execution, review, provider health, and evidence without losing the system-level picture. They are not browsing casually; they are steering an always-on engineering runtime.

### Brand Personality
Engineered, rigorous, calm. The interface should feel like a well-built control surface for serious work, not a playful AI toy or a generic dashboard template. The emotional goal is confidence and operational clarity: users should feel that the system is legible, dependable, and under control.

### Aesthetic Direction
Favor a disciplined, systems-oriented visual language over card-heavy AI dashboard tropes. The product should read as a software factory operating surface: intentional hierarchy, strong information density, restrained but decisive accenting, and clear separation between launch, plan, execution, and review contexts. Avoid decorative gradients, novelty metrics, glassmorphism, and interchangeable SaaS admin patterns.

### Design Principles
1. Make control and comprehension feel immediate. Users should understand mission status, active lane, and next action within seconds.
2. Use hierarchy to reduce cognitive drag. Important state changes, blockers, and actions should dominate; supporting detail should stay quiet.
3. Treat every screen as a mode, not a pile of panels. Home, plan, execute, and review should feel meaningfully different in purpose and composition.
4. Prefer structural clarity over ornamental styling. The product should earn trust through organization, rhythm, and precision.
5. Eliminate accidental complexity in the visual system. Repetition, redundant copy, and container-on-container composition should be reduced aggressively.
